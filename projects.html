Projects

Current Projects:

  • Competent multi-objective optimization methods based on evolutionary algorithms and statistical modelling techniques for real-world problems (Collaboration Spain)
    The main objective of this project is to develop methodological and theoretical results for the solution of general and complex multi-objective optimization problems (MOPs) with advanced optimization methods based on evolutionary algorithms and probabilistic graphical models. To advance the state of the art in the methods for MOPs we plan to address the solution of the following questions: 1) Conception of competent multi-objective evolutionary algorithms (Comp-MOEAs) that incorporate probabilistic modeling. 2) Application of Comp-MOEAs to the analysis of human brain recordings with application to the study of neurological diseases and brain computer interfaces. 3) Development of a theoretical model for the analysis of Comp-MOEAs based on the theory of quantitative genetics. 4) Network analysis of probabilistic models learned by Comp-MOEAs with the goal of knowledge extraction. 5) Implementation of software platforms for Comp-MOEAs in Matlab and Python to introduce their use to industrial, teaching, and research applications.
  •  Bio-inspired Multi-objective algorithms: New Strategies and Applications in Software Engineering.
    This project focuses on basic and applied research in the field of ​​   Bio-inspired  Computation. Basic research focuses on a topic that is currently a challenge: new methods to reduce the deterioration of the search algorithms based on Particle Swarm Multiobjective (Multi-Objective Particle Swarm Optimization MOPSO) when applied to problems with many objectives, improving the convergence and diversity. The main objective of this project is to explore the unique characteristics of MOPSO to propose new approaches, some topics such as the selection of leading, multi-swarms, new methods for storing leaders in the external file will be focused. The paper also emphasizes the application of bioinspired algorithms multiobjetivos in Software Engineering (ES) area, assisting in the development and maintenance of software. Project implementation includes a number of stages such as review of literature works, design, implementation and evaluation of algorithms. Expected to contribute to the state of the art in the related field in three main directions: theoretical, empirical studies and implementation of algorithms .

Past Projects:

  • Multi-Objective Particle Swarm Optimization algorithms (MOPSO) and techniques for dealing with Many-Objectives problems. This project focuses in the study of the behavior of MOPSO algorithms in many-objective problems. Different many-objective techniques have been proposed in the literature but few of them have studied the MOPSO behavior. The goal of this project is to study these issues and eventually to propose a technique most suitable for MOPSO. The project includes different empirical analysis to identify the influence of the techniques on the convergence and diversity of MOPSO algorithms using different many-objective problems. The experimental results are compared applying a variety of quality indicators and statistical tests.
  • A Symbolic Fault Prediction Model Based on Multi-objective Particle Swarm Optimization. In the literature the fault-proneness of classes, methods or modules has been used to devise strategies for reducing testing costs and efforts. In general, fault-proneness is predicted through a set of design metrics and, most recently, by using Machine Learning (ML) techniques. However, some ML techniques can not deal with unbalanced data, characteristic very common of the fault datasets and, their produced results are not easily interpreted by most programmers and testers. Considering these facts, this project introduces a novel fault prediction approach based on Multi-objective Particle Swarm Optimization (MOPSO). Exploring Pareto dominance concepts, the approach generates a model composed by rules with specific properties. These rules can be used as an unordered classifier, and, because of this, they are more intuitive and comprehensible. Different experiments will be accomplished, considering respectively fault-proneness of classes and methods.
  • Multi-Objective Particle Swarm Optimization applied in unstructured networks. This project aims to use P2P networks whose nodes are running multi-objective PSO algorithms. This study involves three aspects: 1) the spread of the topology between the nodes, 2) the implementation of optimization itself, and 3) the coordination of the trading activity of the optimization parameters needed between those involved nodes. Thus, we obtain a distributed optimization where you can take advantage of the features of this environment. Such characteristics as fault tolerance, scalability and load balancing are explored to obtain better results from problems with many objectives with regard to the quality of results and time to solve them, because the activity can be performed simultaneously in several nodes independently.
